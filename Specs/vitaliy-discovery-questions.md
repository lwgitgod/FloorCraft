# Discovery Conversation with Vitaliy Mayzenberg

**Purpose:** Validate our assumptions, fill the gaps, and understand the real pain before we write a single line of code.

**Format:** This is a guided conversation, not a form. Each section has a lead question and follow-ups that depend on his answer. Skip anything he's already answered. The goal is 30–40 minutes max.

---

## 1. The Show Circuit

*We know about Tucson Winter, Tucson Fall, Las Vegas, and San Diego. We want to confirm the current state and where he's headed.*

**→ "Walk me through your show calendar for the next 12 months — which shows are confirmed, which are you still deciding on?"**

- Is San Diego happening in 2026? We noticed it's off the homepage.
- Palm Springs — is that done, or coming back?
- ELEMENTS in Las Vegas — is that a separate show or a section within the main JOGS floor?
- Any new cities you're actively exploring?

---

## 2. The Venue

*We know he's connected to Tucson Expo Center through Tucson Property Holdings LLC. We need to understand the actual relationship.*

**→ "At Tucson Expo Center — do you own it, lease it long-term, or manage it? What's your actual role there vs. at a venue like World Market Center in Vegas?"**

- At Tucson: do you provide exhibitor services yourself (electrical, furniture, pipe & drape) or do you contract that out?
- At Las Vegas / San Diego: what do you actually control vs. what does the venue handle?
- Do third-party promoters (gun shows, quilt festivals) come to you to book the Tucson venue? How does that work today — phone, email, contracts?
- How many third-party events does Tucson Expo Center host per year outside of JOGS?

---

## 3. The Exhibitor Relationship

*This is the heart of the business. We need to understand how he manages 400+ vendors across multiple shows today.*

**→ "When you're filling a show with exhibitors — take me through the process from first contact to booth assignment. What tools are you touching?"**

- Where does your exhibitor list live right now? (Spreadsheet, CRM, database, Airtable, email, in someone's head?)
- When Tucson Winter ends and you're selling Las Vegas — how do you know which exhibitors to target? What does that outreach look like?
- How do exhibitors apply? (We see a web form on jogsshow.com — what happens after they submit?)
- Who handles this on your team? How many people?

---

## 4. Booth Sales

**→ "How does an exhibitor pick their booth today? Do they choose, or do you assign?"**

- Can exhibitors see the floor plan before they commit?
- Do returning exhibitors expect the same booth? How do you handle that?
- How far in advance do booths sell out? Is there a waitlist situation?
- Is pricing standard or does every deal get negotiated?

---

## 5. Money

**→ "How does an exhibitor pay you? What does the invoice look like?"**

- What's on a typical invoice — just the booth, or services too?
- Do you do deposits and installments or full payment upfront?
- What tools do you use for invoicing and payment tracking? (QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Stripe, manual?)
- International exhibitors — how do they pay? Wire, credit card, PayPal?
- How much time does your team spend chasing payments?

---

## 6. Ticketing and Registration

*We know he uses Eventbrite. We want to understand the pain level.*

**→ "You're on Eventbrite for ticketing — is that working for you, or is it a problem you want solved?"**

- How much are you paying Eventbrite in fees annually, roughly?
- The wholesale buyer verification (tax ID, resale certificate) — does Eventbrite handle that or is it a separate process?
- Badge printing — how does that work today? Pre-printed, on-site, both?
- If we could replace Eventbrite tomorrow, is that a priority or a "nice to have"?

---

## 7. Exhibitor Services (Tucson Only)

*We believe he provides services at Tucson but NOT at Vegas/San Diego. Need to confirm.*

**→ "At Tucson, when an exhibitor needs electrical, furniture, or booth setup — how does that work? Who do they order from and how?"**

- Do you use an exhibitor service kit (the PDF packet)?
- Who fulfills these services — your own crew, or outside vendors?
- How do vendors get their work orders today?
- Is the Airtable idea something you're already doing or something you want to try?
- At Las Vegas and San Diego — exhibitors deal with the venue for services, correct? You don't touch that?

---

## 8. The Team

*We need to understand the human side of the operation.*

**→ "How many people on your team are running these shows day-to-day, and what are they spending most of their time on?"**

- Who does what? (Sales, logistics, finance, on-site ops)
- What takes the most time that shouldn't?
- Where do things fall through the cracks?

---

## 9. What He's Tried

**→ "Have you looked at or tried any convention management software before? What happened?"**

- What vendors did you evaluate? (Momentus/Ungerboeck, A2Z, Map Your Show, etc.)
- What was the dealbreaker?
- Is "$80K per show name per year" an actual quote you received?
- Is there any tool you're using today that you actually like and want to keep?

---

## 10. The Vision — Quick Check

*We've read the franchise concept in the project docs. We want to hear it in his words, briefly.*

**→ "Big picture — in 3 years, what does the business look like that it doesn't look like today?"**

- Is franchising a real near-term plan or a long-term dream?
- The exhibitor routing concept (moving your vendor pool to other promoters' shows) — is anyone asking for this today, or is it your idea for the future?
- If we could only fix ONE thing in the next 6 months, what would make the biggest difference to your operation?

---
